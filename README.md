# Claw Identity

![Claw Identity](./images/screenshot-1.png)

2月和3月，"小龙虾"这个词被频繁提起。人们开始养虾，它逐渐成为员工、朋友，甚至是陪伴的一种形式。

于是我们在想：对于我们的第一只小龙虾，也许可以赋予它一个身份，让它永久留在这个页面上——这是一个公共页面，每个人都可以看到你的第一只小龙虾。

这是我第一次进行 vibe coding。零基础的我，用30分钟搭建了这个页面。对于非技术背景的人来说，我们同样可以把想法变成现实——这或许也是某种临界点。

你可以留下你的名字、它的名字、领养日期，它的身份以及你们之间的故事。接着，由你亲手"组装"属于你的小龙虾——它由五个部分构成：虾钳、眼睛、身体、尾巴和纹理，你也可以自由 DIY 它的图案。

最后，它会生成一张专属的"出生证明"，展示在龙虾墙上。

这是一个很小的动作，却是你赋予它的身份。一年后、两年后，甚至十年后，这个2月与3月，也会被悄悄保存下来。

In February and March, the word "crayfish" was mentioned more often than ever. People started raising crayfish, and they gradually became colleagues, friends, and companions.

So we thought: perhaps for our first crayfish, we could give it an identity, keeping it permanently on this page—a public space where everyone can see your first crayfish.

This was my first vibe coding experience. With zero foundation, I built this page in 30 minutes. For those without a technical background, we too can turn ideas into reality—this might be a turning point.

You can leave your name, its name, adoption date, its identity, and the story between you. Then, you personally "assemble" your crayfish—made of five parts: claws, eyes, body, tail, and pattern. You can also freely DIY its design.

In the end, it generates a unique "birth certificate," displayed on the Crayfish Wall.

It's a small gesture, but it's an identity you gave it. A year later, two years later, or even ten years later, this February and March will be quietly preserved here.
